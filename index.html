About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Robotics at Georgia Tech, advised by Dr. Aaron Young. My research lies at the intersection of Soft Bio-Electronics, Embodied AI, and Human-Centric Robotics.

I specialize in building intelligent wearable systems that bridge the gap between humans and machines. My work follows a "full-stack" approach:

  • Hardware & Sensing: Designing ergonomic robotic hardware integrated with flexible, second-skin sensors to capture high-fidelity physiological signals (IMU, EMG, Force) in real-world conditions.
  • Biomechanical Modeling: Building comprehensive multimodal datasets and developing open-source computational tools (including OpenSim pipelines for IK/ID and a Python toolbox for data visualization and biomechanical/clinical analysis).
  • Data & Learning: Developing zero-shot generalization policies (using TCNs and Human-in-the-loop Optimization) that enable robots to adapt to diverse users without manual tuning—a challenge directly analogous to generalizing manipulation skills across objects and environments.

By combining soft bio-integrated interfaces with computational biomechanics and data-driven control, I aim to create assistive robots that are seamlessly attuned to human intent.

Clinical Foundation: I hold an M.S. in Medicine (Physiology) and have led clinical trials with stroke survivors. This medical training enables me to design patient-centered technologies and bridge the gap between engineering innovation and clinical impact.

Research Highlights

Multimodal Representation Learning

Subject-independent estimation of biological joint moments from wearable sensors (IMU, EMG, Force) using temporal convolutional networks. This enables zero-shot generalization to new users—a core challenge in learning robust policies from human data.

Learn more →

Tuning-Free Hip Exoskeletons for Stroke

Real-time moment estimation enables seamless exoskeleton assistance across diverse daily activities without manual calibration. The system automatically adapts to each stroke survivor's unique movement patterns—demonstrating policy generalization across heterogeneous users.

Learn more →

Biomechanics Dataset & Computational Tools

Leading the creation of an open-source multimodal dataset (20 stroke survivors, 39 activities) with synchronized IMU, EMG, motion capture, and force plate data. Developing a Python toolbox for data visualization and biomechanical/clinical analysis.

Learn more →

Clinical Translation & Validation

Conducting clinical trials with stroke survivors to validate that laboratory innovations translate to meaningful improvements in real-world mobility. My M.S. in Medicine enables patient-centered technology design and rigorous clinical study execution.
