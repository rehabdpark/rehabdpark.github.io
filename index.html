About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Robotics program at Georgia Institute of Technology, advised by Dr. Aaron Young in the Exoskeleton & Prosthetic Intelligent Controls (EPIC) Lab.

My research centers on deep learning-based estimation of physiological states from wearable sensors to enable intelligent assistive devices. By continuously estimating users' biological joint moments in real-time, I develop versatile, tuning-free exoskeleton controllers that seamlessly adapt to diverse individuals and activities—without manual calibration or task classification. My current work applies this approach to hip exoskeletons for stroke survivors, translating laboratory advances into real-world mobility improvements.

More broadly, I am passionate about leveraging wearable technology, machine learning, and biomechanical modeling to understand and enhance human movement. My interests span data-driven health monitoring, flexible biointerfaces for intervention, and developing computational tools that interpret motion for diverse healthcare applications.

My unique background bridges engineering and medicine: I hold a B.S. in Robotics and an M.S. in Medicine from Yonsei University, where I conducted clinical gait analysis and rehabilitation research at Severance Hospital. This clinical foundation enables me to design technologies grounded in patient needs and translate innovations from bench to bedside.

Research Highlights

Deep Learning for Physiological State Estimation

Subject-independent estimation of biological joint moments from wearable sensors using temporal convolutional networks. This core technology enables versatile, tuning-free assistance by continuously interpreting users' physiological states in real-time.

Learn more →

Tuning-Free Hip Exoskeletons for Stroke

Applying real-time moment estimation to deliver seamless exoskeleton assistance across diverse daily activities—walking, stairs, sit-to-stand—without manual calibration or task classification. The system automatically adapts to each stroke survivor's unique movement patterns.

Learn more →

Clinical Translation & Validation

Conducting clinical trials with stroke survivors to validate that laboratory innovations translate to meaningful improvements in real-world mobility and quality of life. My medical background enables patient-centered technology design.

Learn more →

Comprehensive Stroke Biomechanics Dataset

Leading the creation of an open-source dataset capturing multi-modal wearable sensor data from 20 stroke survivors across 21 daily activities. A unique resource for rehabilitation research, wearable sensor development, and exoskeleton control strategies.
